Hold a key. Speak.
It's typed.

Personal hold-to-talk dictation for macOS — fully local, English + Spanish. No cloud, no accounts. Audio and text never leave the machine.

Get scribe
Hold Right ⌥ and speak — the cleaned-up text lands here.
1
Hold Right ⌥
The mic is captured only while the key is held — the orange indicator turns off at key-up.
2
Speak
Parakeet transcribes in ~0.2 s. Gemma 3 4B strips fillers and resolves self-corrections.
3
Release
Text is pasted into whatever app has focus. If cleanup ever misbehaves, the raw transcript is pasted — you never lose words.

It learns your words.

Cleanup knows English and Spanish — not the names of your projects, tools and teammates. Teach it once, or let it pick them up as you go.

Learned terms
Distinctive vocabulary — proper nouns, acronyms, camelCase — is picked up after it turns up in three separate dictations, then spelled your way from there on. Ordinary words are never learned, and unused terms are forgotten.
Replacements
Correct it once — heard as → replace with — and it sticks. Speech recognition mangles an unfamiliar name differently every time, so one correction also teaches the right spelling and catches the near-misses.

Stored on your Mac as individual words — never transcripts. Turn learning off or clear it whenever you like.

Full privacy. Your conversations go nowhere.

Local models only
Parakeet TDT v3 for speech, Gemma 3 4B for cleanup — downloaded once, then everything runs offline.
Transcripts never saved
History keeps the last 10 dictations in memory only. The one thing written to disk is your dictionary — individual words, never transcripts.
Nothing sent
No cloud, no accounts, no telemetry. Audio and text never leave the machine.
